MSc Thesis
Computational Model of the Peripheral Nervous System

2014 - 2015
Imperial College London supervised by S.R. Schultz and S. Jarvis

Although peripheral nerves stimulation is widely used such as the vagus nerve stimulation in various treatments, the type of fibres recruited for these treatments is not well characterized. The objective was to create a toolbox allowing modelling a peripheral nerve and reproducing the measured signal. The signal generally measured by an electrode around a nerve is the compound action potential, which corresponds to sum the potentials produce by each fibre in the electrode. PNPy is a Python toolbox which models this compound action potential, allowing for recording by multiple electrodes. It uses the NEURON simulation environment to model the myelinated and unmyelinated axons, as well as the extracellular electrode mechanism, and makes use of the LFPy toolbox to compute the local field potential produced by each axon at the pole of the electrodes.
Long term aim was to improve our understanding of the measurement from peripheral nerve electrodes. In particular, this type of model could contribute to the development of a closed-loop bioelectronic device to monitor the effects of a particular stimulation treatment.

Extensive usage of Python, NEURON and LFPy toolbox.
NB: The package was not publicly released due to a following thesis funded by GSK. However, it is available on demand.
Recently (09/2017) an article entitled Multiscale simulation of peripheral neural signaling was submitted on bioRxiv.
